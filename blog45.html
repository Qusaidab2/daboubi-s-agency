Why Most People Stay Broke (And How to Break the Cycle)

Posted on August 8, 2025

Let’s be honest: being broke isn’t always about how much money you make. Often, it’s about how you think, spend, and react to the world around you.

Many people are caught in a cycle that looks like this: earn → spend → stress → repeat. Even those who increase their income often find that their expenses grow to match (or exceed) it. This is called “lifestyle inflation,” and it’s one of the most dangerous traps.

So, why do most people stay broke?

1. Emotional Spending: Buying things to feel better is one of the fastest ways to stay financially stuck. Retail therapy might feel good for a moment, but the high fades quickly, leaving only the bill behind.

2. Lack of Financial Education: Most schools don’t teach how to manage money, budget, or invest. So unless you actively seek it out, you may not even know how to escape the paycheck-to-paycheck loop.

3. Instant Gratification Culture: We live in a world of one-click orders and same-day delivery. The idea of waiting to buy something, or saving up for it, feels foreign. But impulse buys are usually the most regrettable.

4. Keeping Up With Others: Social media pressures us to “look successful.” But flexing with new shoes or a vacation you can’t afford just digs the hole deeper. Real wealth isn’t loud — it’s quiet, disciplined, and often invisible.

How to break the cycle

1. Track Every Dinar (or Dollar): Awareness is the first step. For one month, write down every expense. You’ll be shocked how much slips away unnoticed — coffees, late-night food, apps, subscriptions.

2. Build a Delay Buffer: Before any non-essential purchase, pause. Ask: “Do I need this right now? Or am I chasing a feeling?” Often, a 24-hour wait kills the impulse.

3. Set a Real, Emotional Goal: “Save money” is too vague. But “I want to save 1,000 JOD to take my mom on a trip” lights up your brain emotionally — and you’ll be more likely to follow through.

4. Learn About Money Weekly: Watch one video, read one post, or listen to one podcast about money each week. Small steps = major mindset shifts.

5. Surround Yourself With Growth: If your friends mock savings or brag about debt, it’s time to upgrade your circle. You become who you’re around — financially, mentally, emotionally.

Final thoughts

Being broke doesn’t mean you’re lazy or doomed. But if you keep repeating the same patterns, you’ll keep getting the same results. Change doesn’t happen overnight — but it does happen with small, consistent steps in a new direction.

Choose your future with intention. Your bank account will thank you later.
