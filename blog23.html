How to Make People Want What You Offer (Even If It’s Not Unique)

Posted on July 16, 2025

Let’s be honest — your product or service probably isn’t the only one of its kind. And that’s okay. People don’t buy what’s different — they buy what feels meant for them.

🧲 It’s Not About the Product — It’s About the Perception

Positioning is everything. When people see your offer as the solution to their specific problem, they stop comparing and start wanting.

🧠 Psychological Triggers That Create Desire

  • Specificity: Speak directly to one person, not everyone.
  • Urgency: Add time pressure or exclusivity.
  • Status: Show how your offer elevates them.
  • Emotion: Tap into pain, ambition, or belonging.

💡 Real-World Example

Two people sell the same shoes. One says, “Comfortable sneakers.” The other says, “The shoe worn by 10,000 entrepreneurs who work 12+ hours a day — without foot pain.” Guess which one sells?

You don’t need a new product — you need a new way to make people feel it’s for them.
